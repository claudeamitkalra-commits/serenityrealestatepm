What Else to Consider — Gaps & Blind Spots

🔴 Utility Bill Timing Rule — Process Gap (Confirmed)
Bills paid in February but entered 3/7/26 caused the PPTCLEX $6,939.16 shortfall. There is no enforced rule today requiring same-month entry. A serenity-utility skill should include a hard warning when a bill's payment date is in a prior month. Consider a standing reminder to Saloni at the end of each month: "Have all bills paid this month been entered in Buildium?"
🔴 Reserve Budget Not Formalized
The HVAC reserve was only $1,000 against a $2,800 actual cost — a $1,800 gap. There is no written reserve schedule per property per expense category. A reserve model should be built (HVAC replacement cost ÷ expected life = monthly holdback) and tracked in a spreadsheet. This is especially critical for aging multi-family units at 1844/1854 Augusta.
🟠 Vacancy Impact Not Being Reported to Owners in Real-Time
The PPTCLEX $5,302 income drop from 4 vacant units is an ongoing structural drag, not a one-time event. Currently owners only hear about this in the monthly narrative. Consider a mid-month vacancy alert to PPTCLEX LLC when vacancy income shortfall is expected to exceed $1,000.
🟠 Security Deposit Accounting — Not Automated
Two SD accounts (BofA-0898 TN, WF-9646 KY) hold tenant security deposits. There is no automated reconciliation of deposit held vs. deposits in Buildium. Move-out settlement and SD disposition process is fully manual with no documented SOP or skill.
🟠 No Tenant Communication Templates for NSF / Late Notices
When a payment bounces, there's no standardized tenant-facing communication template for the NSF notice. This risks inconsistent messaging and could have legal implications (state notice requirements differ between TN and KY). A template library for common tenant events (NSF notice, late rent notice, lease renewal offer, move-out instructions) should be documented.
✅ Year-End Process — Covered by serenity-year-end
1099 generation, annual Cash Flow Statements, vendor W-9 collection, and PM holiday gift coordination are now documented and automated via serenity-year-end. KFB insurance renewal tracking is handled via serenity-reserve-management.
🔵 Leasing Commission Clawback Tracking — No Spreadsheet
The SKILL.md defines the clawback rule (tenant must complete lease term) but there is no persistent tracker that flags placements within the last 12 months and checks whether those tenants are still in place. Today this relies on memory. A simple spreadsheet tab in the Mgmt Fee sheet with placement dates and vacate dates would automate the detection.
🔵 Exception Handling Rules Not Backtested for Serenity PM
The Goddard skill has 39 exception handling (EH) rules backtested over 5 months. The serenity-pm-bookkeeping skill has SOPs but no formal EH rule inventory or backtest. Suggest documenting known exception patterns (late fee reversals dominating, zero-draw months, large capital expenses) as named rules in the skill file.
🔵 Owner Narrative — Fine-Tuning Not Yet Completed
Owner narratives (Task 4) and owner draws (Task 5) are marked as "fine-tuning" in the skill — meaning they are NEVER to be sent or processed without explicit Amit approval. There is no documented milestone or criteria for when they will move to "live." Setting a target date and completion checklist would help move these forward.
🔵 No Buildium Data Quality Monitor
Stale lease records, duplicate vendor entries, and unreconciled tenant balances accumulate over time. There is no periodic data quality check (e.g., leases expired with no renewal entered, tenants with negative balances, duplicate payment entries). A quarterly Buildium audit checklist would prevent these from compounding.
