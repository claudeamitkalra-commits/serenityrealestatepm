A little about me

I spent 28 years in industrial supply — started on the warehouse floor pulling orders, worked my way up to regional operations manager. Good job. Honest work. Wore steel toes more years than I wore dress shoes. Along the way, my wife and I scrimped and saved every extra dollar we could. We didn't take the big vacations. We drove the cars a couple extra years. We put that money into rental properties instead — a couple duplexes, a single-family home here and there. The idea was simple: build something we could leave our kids, and something that would take care of us when we were done working.

Since I was still working 50-hour weeks, I hired a property management company. I figured, hey, these are professionals — they'll have my best interest at heart.

They didn't.

Every month there was a new fee I didn't understand. Maintenance markups I wasn't told about. Statements that didn't add up. Vacancies that dragged on way too long. I'd ask questions and get the runaround. The properties my wife and I sacrificed for — the ones we skipped dinners out and new cars to buy — were being treated like line items on somebody else's spreadsheet. Nobody cared about them the way we did.

When I retired, I finally had the time to roll up my sleeves and manage them myself. And you know what? Turns out all those years of running warehouse operations, managing crews, keeping track of inventory and budgets — that's basically property management. Except now I actually give a damn about the outcome. And because my whole career was built on scaling warehouse operations and managing large teams, scaling those exact same systems to manage a 50+ unit apartment complex or a commercial portfolio is second nature.

People started asking me to manage their places too. A buddy from church. A neighbor's mom who inherited a duplex and needed a duplex property manager she could trust. A doctor who'd just bought his first two single-family homes and didn't know where to start with single-family home property management. And every single time, I thought about how I felt when my property manager was nickel-and-diming me, and I promised myself — I'd never do that to someone else.

What we do (and how we do it different)

Look, I'm not going to give you a fancy bullet-point list with stock photos of people in suits shaking hands. Here's the deal:

🔎 Tenant Placement We find good tenants. Not fast tenants — good tenants. Background checks, income verification, references. I'd rather a unit sits empty an extra two weeks than put someone in there who's going to trash it or stop paying.
💰 Rent Collection Tenants pay on time or they hear from us. Simple as that. You get your money deposited, with a clear statement showing every dollar in and every dollar out. No mysteries.
🔧 Maintenance I've spent my career finding guys who know how to get things done — and get them done right, the first time, at a fair price. No $400 invoices for a $40 fix. We don't mark up maintenance. The plumber's bill is the plumber's bill.
📈 Monthly Reporting This is where I'll brag a little. My background in operations means I'm borderline obsessive about the numbers. Every owner gets a detailed monthly statement that I personally write and review. You'll know exactly where every penny went. If something looks off, I want you to call me on it. That's how it should work.
🙏 Honest Communication You'll have my cell phone number. Not a call center. Not a ticket system. My number. I pick up. If I can't, I call back — same day. Every time.

Here's what I won't do

  • I won't charge you fees I haven't explained to you first
  • I won't mark up maintenance work and pocket the difference
  • I won't let a vacancy drag on because filling it isn't urgent to me
  • I won't send you a statement that I haven't personally reviewed
  • I won't treat your property like it's just another account number

I know what it feels like to work your whole life for something and then hand it to somebody who treats it like it doesn't matter. I won't do that to you.
